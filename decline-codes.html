Module 03

Common Decline Codes

Learn how to interpret common card decline responses and guide merchants toward the right next step.

🚫
→
🏦

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you should be able to...

  • Explain the difference between hard and soft declines.
  • Identify common decline codes and what they mean.
  • Tell merchants when to retry, when to stop, and when to request another payment method.
  • Explain pending holds caused by AVS or CVV gateway declines.
  • Recognize when a decline comes from the issuing bank versus the gateway.

What is a decline code?

When a transaction fails, the issuing bank, processor, or gateway may return a response code explaining why the transaction did not complete. These codes help support teams decide what the merchant should do next.

Support Tip: A decline code does not always mean something is wrong with the terminal. Many declines come directly from the customer’s issuing bank.

Quick Reference

Hard Declines vs. Soft Declines

Soft Declines Hard Declines
Usually temporary or fixable. Usually permanent or serious.
May succeed after correcting the issue. Should not be repeatedly retried.
Examples: 51, 91, N7 Examples: 04, 41, 43

Hard Declines

Do not keep retrying

05

Do Not Honor

The issuing bank declined the transaction but does not provide a specific reason.

Merchant Action: Ask for another payment method. The customer may need to contact their bank.

04

Pick Up Card

The card may be reported lost, stolen, or otherwise restricted.

Merchant Action: Stop attempting the card and request another payment method.

14

Invalid Card Number

The card number entered does not match a valid account.

Merchant Action: Recheck manually entered numbers or request another card.

54

Expired Card

The card is expired or the expiration date was entered incorrectly.

Merchant Action: Verify the expiration date or request another payment method.

Soft Declines

May be fixable

51

Insufficient Funds

The customer does not have enough available funds or credit for the purchase.

Merchant Action: Ask for another payment method or have the customer contact their bank.

Support Note: A 51 may be recoverable if the customer adds funds, but repeatedly retrying the same declined transaction can violate card network retry rules.
N7

CVV Failure

The security code did not match the issuing bank’s records.

Merchant Action: Re-enter the CVV carefully or request another payment method.

62

Restricted Card

The card may be restricted by merchant type, location, currency, MCC, or issuer controls.

Merchant Action: Ask for another payment method or have the customer contact their bank.

91

Issuer Unavailable

The issuing bank or network could not respond at the time of the transaction.

Merchant Action: Wait briefly and try again, or request another payment method.

Card-Not-Present

AVS and CVV checks

Online, phone, invoice, and virtual terminal payments often rely on additional fraud checks. AVS compares the billing address or ZIP code to the issuing bank’s records. CVV verifies the security code printed on the card.

Important: A transaction can receive issuer approval but still be declined by gateway fraud settings.

Support Alert

The Gateway vs. Issuer Trap: “Ghost Authorization”

One of the most confusing support calls happens when a customer sees a pending charge in their banking app, but the merchant’s gateway shows the transaction as declined.

  • The issuing bank approved the authorization and placed a temporary hold on the customer’s available funds.
  • The gateway rejected the transaction before capture because of fraud rules such as AVS or CVV mismatch.
  • The merchant was not paid because the transaction was never captured.
  • The pending hold should automatically disappear when the issuing bank releases it.
Support Tip: Explain that the customer was not permanently charged. Do not advise the merchant to ship goods unless there is a successful captured transaction.

Troubleshooting Mindset

Ask these three questions

  • Who declined the transaction? Issuer, gateway, processor, or local device?
  • Is it temporary or permanent? Soft declines may be fixable; hard declines usually are not.
  • What should the merchant do next? Retry later, correct data, request another card, or have the customer call their bank.

Common Misconceptions

What new reps often get wrong

  • ❌ Every decline is a terminal problem.
    ✅ No. Many declines come from the issuing bank or gateway fraud rules.
  • ❌ A pending hold means the merchant got paid.
    ✅ No. Pending authorizations are not the same as captured funds.
  • ❌ A 05 can be overridden by support.
    ✅ No. Issuer declines cannot be forced through by the processor or support team.
  • ❌ Soft declines can be retried forever.
    ✅ No. Repeated retries can create network compliance issues.

Support Desk Simulations

Diagnose the decline

1. An e-commerce merchant says an angry customer was charged three times, but the gateway shows all three attempts declined due to AVS mismatch. The customer sees three pending charges. What is happening?

✅ Correct! This is a ghost authorization. The issuing bank placed temporary holds, but the gateway blocked capture due to AVS mismatch.

❌ Not quite. The customer is seeing temporary authorization holds, not completed charges.

2. A merchant receives a 05 - Do Not Honor decline and asks support to override it. What should support say?

✅ Correct! A 05 comes from the customer’s issuing bank and cannot be overridden by support.

❌ Not quite. A 05 is an issuer-level decline, not a terminal or gateway setting issue.

3. A cashier runs the same card six times after receiving a 51 - Insufficient Funds decline. Why is this a problem?

✅ Correct! A 51 may be recoverable, but repeatedly retrying the same decline is not good practice.

❌ Not quite. The issue is excessive retry behavior, not hardware, billing, or PCI scope.

4. Which code represents a true hard decline where the merchant should stop retrying and request another payment method?

✅ Correct! A 04 means the card may be lost, stolen, or restricted. Stop retrying.

❌ Not quite. A 04 is the true hard stop in this list.

5. A corporate card declines with 62 - Restricted Card, even though it is active with plenty of available credit. What is the most likely explanation?

✅ Correct! Restricted cards may be limited by MCC, location, currency, or issuer-defined controls.

❌ Not quite. A 62 usually means the card is healthy but restricted by issuer controls.

Module 03

Common Decline Codes

When a transaction fails, the issuing bank sends back a response code. Understanding those codes helps merchants know whether to retry, fix entered data, ask for another card, or have the customer call their bank.

❌
→
💳

Why decline codes matter

A decline code is more than an error message. It tells the merchant what kind of problem occurred and what the next best action should be.

Support Tip: Not every decline should be retried. Repeatedly retrying the same card can create fraud concerns and frustrate the customer.

Hard Declines

Do Not Retry

Hard declines are usually permanent for that transaction. The merchant should ask for another payment method or have the customer contact their issuing bank.

05

Do Not Honor

The issuer declined the transaction without giving a specific reason.

Merchant Action:

Ask for another payment method. The customer must call their bank.

04

Pick Up Card

The card may be lost, stolen, or blocked by the issuer.

Merchant Action:

Do not retry. Follow company policy.

14

Invalid Card Number

The card number does not exist or was entered incorrectly.

Merchant Action:

Verify the card number or ask for another card.

54

Expired Card

The expiration date is expired or was entered incorrectly.

Merchant Action:

Verify the expiration date or use another payment method.

Soft Declines

Usually Fixable

Soft declines are often caused by temporary issues, incorrect information, or issuer/network communication problems.

51

Insufficient Funds

The account does not have enough available credit or cash.

Merchant Action:

Ask for another card, a lower amount, or for the customer to try later.

N7

CVV Failure

The security code was entered incorrectly or failed validation.

Merchant Action:

Re-enter the CVV carefully or ask for another payment method.

62

Restricted Card

The card may be blocked for this merchant type, region, or transaction category.

Merchant Action:

Ask for a different card or have the customer contact their bank.

91

Issuer Unavailable

A temporary communication issue occurred between systems.

Merchant Action:

Wait a moment and try again.

Card-Not-Present Checks

AVS, CVV, and fraud settings

Online, phone, invoice, and keyed transactions often rely on additional security checks because the card is not physically present.

  • AVS: Compares the billing address or ZIP code to the issuing bank’s records.
  • CVV: Verifies the 3- or 4-digit card security code.
  • Gateway Rules: Some systems decline transactions automatically based on AVS, CVV, velocity, or risk settings.
Good to Know: A transaction can receive issuer approval but still be declined by gateway fraud settings.

Support Insight

“Do Not Honor” cannot be overridden.

If a merchant receives a 05 decline, support agents cannot force the approval. The customer needs to contact the issuing bank or use another payment method.

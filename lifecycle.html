Module 02

Transaction Lifecycle

Every card payment looks simple on the surface: tap, swipe, approved. Behind that moment is a full lifecycle that determines whether the merchant actually gets paid.

Authorization

Authorization begins when a customer taps, inserts, swipes, or enters card information. The request travels from the merchant’s terminal or payment software through the processor/acquirer, card network, and issuing bank.

Customer → Terminal / POS → Processor / Acquirer → Card Network → Issuing Bank
Remember: Authorization success does not mean the merchant has been paid. It only means the issuer approved the transaction.

Clearing

After authorization, approved transactions are submitted for clearing, often in daily batches. This is where finalized transaction details are exchanged and the payment promise is reconciled.

Settlement & Funding

Settlement is when funds move between financial institutions. Funding is when the merchant receives the deposit, usually within 1–2 business days depending on processor schedule, batch timing, weekends, holidays, and risk holds.

Support Insight

Payments do not end at “approved.”

They end when the money lands, reconciles, and stays there. This distinction helps support teams troubleshoot funding delays, batch issues, settlement questions, and merchant confusion.

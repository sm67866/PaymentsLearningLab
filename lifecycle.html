Module 02

Transaction Lifecycle

Follow a card payment from authorization to merchant funding, and learn how to diagnose common questions about missing deposits, batch timing, and settlement delays.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you should be able to...

  • Explain the difference between authorization and capture.
  • Understand how batching starts the funding process.
  • Describe the difference between clearing, settlement, and funding.
  • Explain processor cutoff times and funding schedules.
  • Diagnose common merchant funding questions.

Phase 1: Moving the Data

1. Authorization

Authorization is the real-time request asking the issuing bank whether a transaction should be approved or declined. It verifies that the card is valid and that the customer has available funds or credit.

Remember: Authorization does not mean the merchant has been paid. It only places a temporary hold on the customer’s funds.

Phase 1: Moving the Data

2. Capture

Capture is the merchant’s official claim on an approved authorization. Without capture, a transaction cannot move forward into batching, clearing, settlement, or funding.

  • Retail transactions often authorize and capture at the same time.
  • Restaurants, hospitality, and e-commerce may capture later.
  • If an authorization expires before capture, the merchant may need to re-run the card.

Phase 2: End of Day Processing

3. Batching

Approved and captured transactions are grouped into a batch. Most merchants close batches automatically at the end of the day, but some merchants manually close their batch from the terminal, POS, or gateway.

Support Tip: If a batch is not closed, the funding clock usually does not start.

The Clock

Processor Cutoff Times

Every processor has a daily cutoff time that determines when captured transactions enter the nightly processing cycle. Transactions batched after the cutoff typically roll into the next business day’s processing window.

Support Tip: Missing the cutoff by even a few minutes can delay the merchant’s next funding cycle.

Funding Schedules

Not every merchant is funded the same way

  • Next Day Funding (NDF): Funds are typically deposited on the next eligible business day after batch processing.
  • Same Day Funding: Some processors offer accelerated funding for qualifying merchants, often for an additional fee.
  • Standard Funding: Funding may take two or more business days depending on the processor, risk profile, bank, weekends, or holidays.
Important: Funding speed depends on the merchant’s funding program and processor setup, not simply on whether the transaction was approved.

Phase 3: Backend Processing

4. Clearing & Settlement

Clearing and settlement usually happen behind the scenes after the batch is submitted. They are closely related, but they are not the same thing.

  • Clearing: Transaction data is exchanged and reconciled between financial institutions.
  • Settlement: Actual funds move from the issuing side to the acquiring side.
  • Fees: Interchange, assessments, and processor markup may reduce the amount deposited to the merchant.
Remember: Clearing moves information. Settlement moves money.

Funding Math

Gross vs. Net Funding

Net Funding

The processor deducts processing fees before depositing funds into the merchant’s bank account.

Example: A $5,000 batch may result in a $4,875 deposit if fees are deducted daily.

Gross Funding

The merchant receives the full batch amount daily, and processing fees are drafted separately, often once per month.

Support Tip: When a merchant asks why their deposit is smaller than their sales total, first verify whether they are on Net Funding or Gross Funding.

Phase 4: The Finish Line

5. Funding

Funding is when the merchant finally receives the deposit into their business bank account. This is the stage merchants usually care about most.

Remember: Settlement is bank-to-bank. Funding is when the merchant actually sees the money.

Support Tool

Where is the money?

Status Where is the money? What should support explain?
Authorized Only Temporary hold at the issuing bank The transaction still needs to be captured before it can move forward.
Captured / Batched Processor queue The batch is closed and waiting for the nightly processing window.
Settled Moving bank-to-bank The funds are in transit and should follow the merchant’s funding schedule.
Funded Merchant bank account The deposit has landed. Reconcile against reporting and fees.

Common Misconceptions

What new reps often get wrong

  • ❌ Authorization means the merchant got paid.
    ✅ No. Authorization only reserves funds.
  • ❌ Settlement and funding are the same thing.
    ✅ No. Settlement moves money between banks. Funding deposits money to the merchant.
  • ❌ Closing a batch instantly creates funding.
    ✅ No. Funding depends on cutoff times, funding schedules, weekends, holidays, and risk holds.
  • ❌ Every merchant has Next Day Funding.
    ✅ No. Funding programs vary by processor, risk, underwriting, and merchant setup.

Support Desk Simulations

Diagnose the merchant issue

1. A restaurant merchant’s reports match their receipts, but they have not received deposits for three days. The gateway shows hundreds of transactions in “Authorized” status. What is the most likely cause?

✅ Correct! Authorized transactions must be captured and batched before they can move to clearing, settlement, and funding.

❌ Not quite. Since the transactions are still Authorized, they have not moved forward into capture and batching yet.

2. An online merchant processed $5,000 in sales, but only $4,875 landed in their bank account. What is the most likely explanation?

✅ Correct! With Net Funding, processing fees are deducted before the daily deposit is sent.

❌ Not quite. A deposit slightly lower than the sales total is commonly explained by Net Funding.

3. A retail merchant closes their batch at 11:30 PM, which is 30 minutes after their processor's daily cutoff time. The merchant calls the next morning asking why their funding appears delayed. What is the most likely explanation?

✅ Correct! Missing the processor's cutoff does not usually cancel the batch. It delays when processing begins.

❌ Not quite. Missing the cutoff usually means the batch rolls into the next processing window. The exact funding date depends on the merchant's funding program, weekends, holidays, processor policies, and bank posting times.

4. During which phase does actual liquidity move bank-to-bank from the customer’s issuing bank to the merchant’s acquiring bank?

✅ Correct! Settlement is when funds move between financial institutions.

❌ Not quite. Clearing moves transaction data. Settlement moves the money.

5. An e-commerce merchant tries to capture an authorization from 10 days ago, but the system will not allow it. What should support explain?

✅ Correct! Authorizations only hold funds for a limited time. Once expired, the merchant must obtain a new authorization.

❌ Not quite. If an authorization expires before capture, the hold drops and the merchant cannot claim those funds without a new authorization.

Module 02

Transaction Lifecycle

Every card payment looks simple on the surface: tap, swipe, approved. Behind that moment is a full lifecycle that determines whether the merchant actually gets paid.

Authorization

Authorization begins when a customer taps, inserts, swipes, or enters card information. The request travels from the merchant’s terminal or payment software through the processor/acquirer, card network, and issuing bank.

Customer → Terminal / POS → Processor / Acquirer → Card Network → Issuing Bank
Remember: Authorization success does not mean the merchant has been paid. It only means the issuer approved the transaction.

Batch

Approved transactions are grouped together in a batch. Some systems close batches automatically at the end of the day, while others require the merchant to close the batch manually.

Support Tip: If the batch has not closed, funding may be delayed.

Clearing

Clearing is when finalized transaction details are exchanged between the processor, card network, and issuing bank. This step reconciles the payment promise made during authorization.

Think of it this way: Clearing confirms what happened before funds move.

Settlement

Settlement is when funds move between financial institutions. The issuing bank, card network, processor, and acquiring bank reconcile the transaction and exchange funds.

Remember: Settlement is bank-to-bank. The merchant may still be waiting for funding.

Funding

Funding is when the merchant receives the deposit into their bank account. This usually happens 1–2 business days after the transaction, depending on batch timing, weekends, holidays, processor schedules, and account holds.

Remember: Funding is the finish line for the merchant.

Support Insight

Payments do not end at “approved.”

They end when the money lands, reconciles, and stays there. This distinction helps support teams troubleshoot funding delays, batch issues, settlement questions, and merchant confusion.

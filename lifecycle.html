Module 02

Transaction Lifecycle

Every card payment looks simple on the surface: tap, swipe, approved. Behind that moment is a full lifecycle that determines whether the merchant actually gets paid.

Phase 1: Moving the Data

1. Authorization

Authorization is a real-time request to verify that the cardholder has sufficient funds or credit. The request travels instantly through the payment chain.

Customer / Card → Terminal / POS → Processor / Acquirer → Card Network → Issuing Bank
Crucial Note: An approval only places a temporary hold on the customer’s funds. It does not mean the merchant has been paid, or that the money has left the customer’s account.

Phase 1: Moving the Data

2. Capture

Capture is the merchant’s official claim on an approved authorization. Without capture, a transaction does not move forward into batching.

  • In retail or grocery, authorization and capture often happen at the same time.
  • In restaurants, hospitality, or e-commerce, capture may happen later.
  • An uncaptured authorization can expire, causing the merchant to lose the sale.

Phase 2: Processing the Money

3. Batching

Approved and captured transactions are stored in a batch. At the end of the business day, the merchant closes the batch automatically through software or manually on the terminal.

Support Tip: If a batch is not closed, the funding clock usually does not start. Forgetting to close a manual batch is a common cause of delayed funding calls.

Phase 2: Processing the Money

4. Clearing & Settlement

Once the processor receives the batch, the backend financial exchange begins. Clearing and settlement are closely related behind-the-scenes processes.

  • Clearing: Transaction data is exchanged so the parties can reconcile who owes what.
  • Settlement: Funds move between the issuing side and acquiring side.
  • Fees: Interchange, assessments, and processor markup reduce the gross amount before the merchant is funded.

Phase 3: The Finish Line

5. Funding

Funding is when the processor or acquiring bank deposits the remaining net funds into the merchant’s business bank account.

Remember: Funding usually happens within 1–2 business days after batch close, but weekends, bank holidays, funding schedules, instant funding options, and risk holds can affect timing.

Support Insight

Payments do not end at “approved.”

They end when the money lands, reconciles, and stays there. Understanding the difference between authorization, capture, batching, clearing, settlement, and funding helps support teams troubleshoot merchant questions with confidence.

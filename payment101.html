Payment Processing 101

A beginner-friendly overview of how card payments move from swipe, dip, or tap to merchant funding.

What is payment processing?

Payment processing is the system that allows a merchant to accept card payments from customers. Behind every approved transaction, several parties communicate in seconds to verify the card, approve the purchase, and eventually move funds to the merchant.

The Main Players

Customer

The person paying with a credit or debit card.

Merchant

The business accepting the payment.

Payment Terminal

The device or software used to collect card information.

Processor

The company that routes transaction data between banks and card networks.

Card Network

Visa, Mastercard, Discover, or American Express.

Issuing Bank

The customer’s bank that approves or declines the transaction.

Simple Transaction Flow

  1. Customer presents a card.
  2. The terminal captures and encrypts the payment data.
  3. The processor routes the transaction request.
  4. The card network sends the request to the issuing bank.
  5. The issuing bank approves or declines the transaction.
  6. The response travels back to the merchant.
  7. Approved transactions are later batched, settled, and funded.

Why this matters

Support teams need to understand the payment flow so they can troubleshoot issues like declines, batch problems, funding delays, terminal errors, and merchant confusion.

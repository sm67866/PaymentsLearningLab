Payment Processing 101

Learn the key players involved in a card transaction and how payment data moves from customer to issuing bank.

Why this matters

Every payment transaction—whether it succeeds or fails—follows the same basic process. When merchants call with questions about declines, funding delays, connection errors, or chargebacks, understanding the payment ecosystem makes troubleshooting dramatically easier.

Key Concepts

Meet the Players

👤

Cardholder

The person or business making a purchase using a credit or debit card.

🏪

Merchant

The business accepting the card payment.

📟

Terminal / POS

The hardware or software used to capture payment information.

🌐

Gateway

Used for online, virtual terminal, and other card-not-present payments.

⚙️

Processor

Routes transaction data between merchants, networks, and financial institutions.

🏦

Acquiring Bank

The merchant-side bank that helps receive settled funds.

🌎

Card Network

Visa, Mastercard, Discover, and American Express route data and set operating rules.

🏛️

Issuing Bank

The customer’s bank. It approves or declines the authorization request.

Simple Transaction Flow

Customer → Terminal / POS → Processor → Card Network → Issuing Bank
Remember: Authorization does not mean the merchant has been paid. Approval only means the issuing bank authorized the transaction. The money moves later through batching, clearing, settlement, and funding.

Common Misconceptions

What new reps often get wrong

  • ❌ Visa approves transactions.
    ✅ No. Card networks route information. The issuing bank approves or declines.
  • ❌ Approved means paid.
    ✅ No. Approval only means the transaction was authorized.
  • ❌ Every payment uses a gateway.
    ✅ No. Gateways are primarily used for online, virtual terminal, and integrated software payments.
  • ❌ The processor owns the customer’s money.
    ✅ No. The issuing bank controls the customer’s account.

Support Tip

Merchant says: “It approved, but I don’t have my money.”

Your first thought should be the transaction lifecycle. Has the batch closed? When was the transaction processed? Is funding scheduled? Is today a banking holiday?

Knowledge Check

Payment Processing 101 Quiz

1. Who ultimately decides whether a transaction is approved or declined?

Correct: The issuing bank owns the customer account and decides whether to approve or decline.

2. Which player acts as the central highway between the processor and issuing bank?

Correct: Card networks route transaction data and set operating rules.

3. True or False: “Approved” means the merchant has already been funded.

Correct: Approval only means authorization occurred. Funding happens later.

4. A terminal says “Connection Error.” What should support investigate first?

Correct: A connection error usually begins at the terminal, POS, network, or local setup.

5. Which player ultimately receives settled funds before the merchant is funded?

Correct: The acquiring side receives settled funds before merchant funding occurs.
